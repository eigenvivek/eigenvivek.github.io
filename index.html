I am a PhD student in Medical Engineering and Medical Physics at the Harvard-MIT Program in Health Sciences and Technology. My current research interests lie in the development of machine learning methods for the diagnosis and treatment of cardiovascular disease.

My past research has focused on the development of computational methods to analyze multi-subject neuroimaging and cardiovascular data. Most recently, I developed statistical graph theory algorithms to perform biomarker discovery in network-valued maps of the brain (advised by Dr. Joshua T. Vogelstein and Dr. Carey E. Priebe). I also led a Design Team of undergraduate biomedical engineers to build a dynamic fusion image guidance system for minimally invasive heart surgery (advised by Dr. Elizabeth Logsdon and Dr. Rani Hasan).
