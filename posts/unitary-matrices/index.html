A Fun Interpretation of Unitary Matrices

2022-05-28 | Why are the rows orthonormal?

This is a proof that if the columns of the matrix are orthonormal, then the rows are also orthonormal.

Let U∈Mn(C)U \in M_n(\mathbb{C})U∈Mn​(C) be a square matrix such that its columns form an orthonormal basis over Cn\mathbb{C}^nCn. That is, for each pair of columns xix_ixi​ and xjx_jxj​, the inner product xi∗xj=δijx_i^* x_j = \delta_{ij}xi∗​xj​=δij​.[1] If we think about matrix multiplication as a series of inner products between the rows of the first matrix and the columns of the second matrix, then this gives us the following identities:

(U∗U)ij=xi∗xj=δij⟹U∗U=I⟹UU∗=I ,(U^*U)_{ij} = x_i^* x_j = \delta_{ij} \Longrightarrow U^*U = I \Longrightarrow UU^* = I \,,(U∗U)ij​=xi∗​xj​=δij​⟹U∗U=I⟹UU∗=I,

where the last identity follows from the fact that if AB=IAB=IAB=I, then BA=IBA=IBA=I.[2] But this is interesting... the last identity implies that the rows of UUU are also orthonormal! If the columns of the matrix are orthonormal, then why are the rows also orthonormal?

For a pair of orthonormal vectors xix_ixi​ and xjx_jxj​, the rank-one matrix Pxi=xixi∗ P_{x_i} = x_i x_i^* Pxi​​=xi​xi∗​ represents the orthogonal projection onto the vector xix_ixi​. Therefore,

UU∗=∑i=1nxixi∗=Px1+⋯+Pxn=I . UU^* = \sum_{i=1}^n x_i x_i^* = P_{x_1} + \cdots + P_{x_n} = I \,. UU∗=i=1∑n​xi​xi∗​=Px1​​+⋯+Pxn​​=I.

Here's why the last equality holds: since the vectors {x1,…,xn}\{x_1, \dots, x_n\}{x1​,…,xn​} form a basis over Cn\mathbb{C}^nCn, the sum of nnn rank-one projection matrices forms a projection into Cn .\mathbb C^n \,.Cn. implying that

This proof shows that orthonormal columns imply orthonormal rows, giving another intuition for the geometric behavior of unitary matrices.
